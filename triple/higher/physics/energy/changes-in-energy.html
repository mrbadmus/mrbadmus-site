⚡ Calculating Changes in Energy

Spec 4.1.1 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

Kinetic Energy — The Energy of Motion

Every moving object has kinetic energy.

The faster it moves, the more KE it has.

Double the speed → FOUR times the KE (because speed is squared).

Double the mass → double the KE (directly proportional).

Gravitational Potential Energy — The Energy of Height

Any object above the ground has GPE.

The higher it is, the more GPE it has.

GPE depends on mass, gravitational field strength (g = 10 N/kg on Earth) and height.

This is why a heavy book falling from a high shelf does more damage than the same book from a low shelf!

Elastic Potential Energy — The Energy of Springs

When you stretch or squash a spring beyond its natural length, it stores elastic potential energy.

The more you extend it, the more energy is stored.

But only works if the spring hasn't been stretched past its elastic limit — after that it won't spring back!

The Specific Heat Capacity Link

When energy is transferred to a substance by heating, the temperature rises.

The equation E = mcΔθ tells us how much energy is needed.

A higher specific heat capacity (c) means more energy to change temperature by 1°C.

Water has a very high specific heat capacity (4200 J/kg/°C) — that's why oceans warm up slowly!

⚠️ Common Mistake

In KE = ½mv², students forget to square the velocity FIRST, then multiply. Write it as KE = ½ × m × (v × v). Also — g = 10 N/kg on Earth (sometimes 9.8 N/kg — use whatever the question gives you).

📐 Variables
KEKinetic Energy (KE) is measured in Joules (J)
mMass (m) is measured in Kilograms (kg)
vSpeed (v) is measured in Metres per second (m/s)
GPEGravitational Potential Energy (GPE) is measured in Joules (J)
gGravitational field strength (g) is measured in Newtons per kg (N/kg)
hHeight (h) is measured in Metres (m)
EEnergy (E) is measured in Joules (J)
cSpecific heat capacity (c) is measured in J/kg/°C (J/kg/°C)
ΔθTemperature change (Δθ) is measured in Degrees Celsius (°C)
📐 Key Equations
KE = ½mv²
GPE = mgh
E = mcΔθ
📌 Key Note

g = 10 N/kg on Earth (use this unless the question states otherwise)

⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Example 1 — Kinetic Energy

A car of mass 1200 kg travels at 20 m/s. Calculate its kinetic energy.

F

KE = ½ × m × v²

I

KE = ½ × 1200 × 20²

F

20² = 400. KE = ½ × 1200 × 400 = 0.5 × 480,000

A

KE = 240,000 J = 240 kJ

Example 2 — Gravitational Potential Energy

A 5 kg ball is lifted to a height of 8 m. Calculate the gain in GPE. (g = 10 N/kg)

F

GPE = m × g × h

I

GPE = 5 × 10 × 8

F

No unit conversion needed

A

GPE = 400 J

Example 3 — Specific Heat Capacity

2 kg of water is heated from 20°C to 70°C. c = 4200 J/kg/°C. Calculate the energy transferred.

F

E = m × c × Δθ

I

Δθ = 70 − 20 = 50°C. E = 2 × 4200 × 50

F

No unit conversion needed

A

E = 420,000 J = 420 kJ

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Elastic potential energy: E = ½ke² where k = spring constant (N/m) and e = extension (m). Doubling extension quadruples elastic PE.
