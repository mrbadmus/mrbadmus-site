Series and Parallel Circuits

Spec 6.2.2 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

Series Circuits

In a SERIES circuit, all components are in a SINGLE LOOP — one path for current.
Rules:
CURRENT: same through every component — I₁ = I₂ = I₃
PD: splits between components — V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃
RESISTANCE: adds up — R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
EXAMPLE:
R₁ = 4 Ω, R₂ = 6 Ω, battery = 10 V.
R_total = 10 Ω. I = 10 ÷ 10 = 1 A.
V₁ = 1 × 4 = 4 V; V₂ = 1 × 6 = 6 V. Check: 4 + 6 = 10 V ✓
If one component breaks — ALL components stop working.

Parallel Circuits

In a PARALLEL circuit, components are in SEPARATE BRANCHES — multiple paths for current.
Rules:
PD: same across every branch — V₁ = V₂ = V₃ = V_supply
CURRENT: splits — I_total = I₁ + I₂ + I₃
RESISTANCE: total is LESS than the smallest individual resistance.
EXAMPLE:
12 V supply. R₁ = 6 Ω, R₂ = 4 Ω.
I₁ = 12 ÷ 6 = 2 A; I₂ = 12 ÷ 4 = 3 A.
I_total = 2 + 3 = 5 A.
If one branch breaks — OTHER branches continue working.

Why Household Circuits Are Parallel

Domestic wiring is PARALLEL because:
1. Each appliance gets the FULL mains pd (230 V).
2. Appliances work INDEPENDENTLY — switching one off doesn't affect others.
3. Easy to add appliances without affecting existing ones.
4. Each appliance can have its own fuse.
SECOMPARISON:
Series: same current, pd splits, R adds, all-or-nothing.
Parallel: same pd, current splits, R decreases, independent operation.
⚠️ Common Mistake

In PARALLEL, total resistance is LESS than any individual resistance — more branches = more paths = lower resistance. Adding resistors in SERIES always increases total resistance. Students often mix these up.

📐 Variables
ICurrent (I) is measured in amperes (A)
VPotential difference (V) is measured in volts (V)
RResistance (R) is measured in ohms (Ω)
📐 Key Equations
Series: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
Series: V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃
Parallel: I_total = I₁ + I₂ + I₃
📌 Key Note

Series: same I, pd splits, R adds. Parallel: same pd, I splits, total R less than smallest. Household = parallel — full voltage, independent. Series break = all stop. Parallel break = others continue.

⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Series Circuit

R₁ = 3 Ω and R₂ = 7 Ω in series with a 20 V battery. Find the current and pd across each resistor.

F

R_total = R₁ + R₂; I = V ÷ R_total; V₁ = I × R₁; V₂ = I × R₂

I

R_total = 3 + 7 = 10 Ω; V = 20 V

F

I = 20 ÷ 10 = 2 A; V₁ = 2 × 3 = 6 V; V₂ = 2 × 7 = 14 V

A

I = 2 A throughout; V₁ = 6 V; V₂ = 14 V (check: 6 + 14 = 20 V ✓)
