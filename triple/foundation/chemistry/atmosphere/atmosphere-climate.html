🧪 The Atmosphere and Climate Change

Spec 4.9 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

Current Composition

Nitrogen (N₂): ~78%. Oxygen (O₂): ~21%. Argon (Ar): ~1%.

Carbon dioxide (CO₂): ~0.04% (increasing due to human activity).

Plus water vapour and trace gases.

Proportions have been roughly stable for ~200 million years.

Evolution of the Atmosphere

Early Earth: volcanoes released CO₂, water vapour, N₂. Very little or no oxygen.

Oceans formed as water vapour condensed.

Photosynthesis (early algae/plants): consumed CO₂, released O₂ → oxygen levels rose.

Carbon locked in fossil fuels (dead organisms compressed over millions of years).

The Greenhouse Effect

Greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane CH₄, water vapour H₂O) absorb outgoing infrared radiation.

Natural greenhouse effect: keeps Earth warm enough for life (~+33°C above no-atmosphere temperature).

Enhanced greenhouse effect: burning fossil fuels, deforestation, farming → more CO₂ and CH₄.

More greenhouse gas → more IR absorbed → Earth warms further → climate change.

Consequences and Solutions

Rising temperatures → melting ice → rising sea levels → flooding of low-lying land.

More extreme weather events. Disruption to ecosystems.

Solutions: renewable energy, electric vehicles, reforestation, carbon capture, nuclear power, reduce meat consumption.

Carbon footprint = total CO₂ equivalent produced by an activity or person.

⚠️ Common Mistake

The greenhouse effect is NOT inherently bad — without it Earth would be about −18°C. It's the ENHANCED greenhouse effect from human activities that causes climate change. Also: water vapour is actually the most abundant greenhouse gas — not CO₂.

📌 Key Note

Atmosphere: 78% N₂, 21% O₂, 1% Ar, 0.04% CO₂. Greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapour.
