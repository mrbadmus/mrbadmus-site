⚡ Red-Shift and the Big Bang

Spec 4.8.2 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is Red-Shift?

When a light source moves away from us, its light is stretched to longer wavelengths.

Longer wavelengths = shifted toward the red end of the spectrum.

This is called red-shift — and it's the Doppler effect applied to light.

If a source moves toward us, wavelengths are compressed → blue-shift.

Evidence from Galaxies

Almost all galaxies show red-shifted light — they are all moving away from us.

The further away the galaxy, the greater the red-shift — it's moving away faster.

This means the universe is EXPANDING in all directions.

No matter where you are in the universe, everything else appears to be moving away.

The Big Bang Theory

If everything is moving apart now, they must have been closer together in the past.

Extrapolating backward → all matter and energy was in a single, extremely hot, dense point.

About 13.8 billion years ago, this point exploded outward — the Big Bang.

The universe has been expanding and cooling ever since.

The Big Bang is the best-supported explanation for the origin of the universe.

Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation

Shortly after the Big Bang, the universe was incredibly hot — full of radiation.

As it expanded and cooled, this radiation stretched to microwave wavelengths.

This Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation fills all of space today.

First detected in 1965 by Penzias and Wilson — strong evidence for the Big Bang.

It is uniform in all directions — consistent with the universe expanding from a single point.

⚠️ Common Mistake

The Big Bang is NOT an explosion in space — it's an expansion OF space itself. There was no 'before' the Big Bang (time began with it). Also — red-shift tells us galaxies are moving AWAY from us, not toward us. Blue-shift = moving toward us (only seen in very close galaxies).

📌 Key Note

CMB radiation = strongest observational evidence for the Big Bang theory.
