⚡ Density

Spec 4.3.1 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is Density?

Density is how much mass is packed into a given volume.

Think of a packed suitcase vs an empty one — same size, very different mass.

A denser material has more particles crammed into the same space.

Density explains why iron sinks in water but wood floats — iron is denser than water, wood is less dense.

Why Solids Are Usually Denser Than Gases

In a solid, particles are tightly packed — lots of mass in a small volume = high density.

In a gas, particles are widely spaced — same mass spread over a huge volume = very low density.

Liquid density is usually close to solid density (particles still close together).

Measuring Density in the Lab

Regular shape (cube/cuboid): measure mass with balance, volume = length × width × height.

Irregular shape: use displacement — submerge in water and measure water level rise.

Volume displaced = volume of object.

Density = mass ÷ volume displaced.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Units! Density in kg/m³ uses kg and m³. If mass is in grams and volume in cm³, density is in g/cm³. DON'T mix units — convert first. Also watch out for converting cm³ to m³: 1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³.

📐 Variables
ρDensity (ρ) is measured in Kilograms per cubic metre (kg/m³)
mMass (m) is measured in Kilograms (kg)
VVolume (V) is measured in Cubic metres (m³)
📐 Key Equations
ρ = m/V
📌 Key Note

Water has density = 1000 kg/m³ (1 g/cm³). Objects less dense than water float; denser objects sink.

⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Example 1 — Calculate density

A block of metal has mass 540 g and volume 200 cm³. Calculate its density in g/cm³.

F

ρ = m ÷ V

I

ρ = 540 ÷ 200

F

Both in consistent units (g and cm³)

A

ρ = 2.7 g/cm³

Example 2 — Find volume

A piece of iron has mass 7800 kg and density 7800 kg/m³. Find its volume.

F

V = m ÷ ρ

I

V = 7800 ÷ 7800

F

No unit conversion needed

A

V = 1 m³
