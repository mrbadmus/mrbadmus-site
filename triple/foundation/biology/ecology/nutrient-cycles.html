🌿 Nutrient Cycles and Biodiversity

Spec 4.7.3 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Carbon Cycle

Carbon moves between the atmosphere, organisms and the Earth in a continuous cycle.

Removed from atmosphere: photosynthesis (plants absorb CO₂).

Returned to atmosphere: respiration (all organisms), decomposition (bacteria/fungi break down dead material), combustion (burning fossil fuels).

Fossil fuels = ancient carbon locked underground — burning releases it → increases atmospheric CO₂.

The Water Cycle

Evaporation: water evaporates from oceans, lakes and plants (transpiration).

Condensation: water vapour cools → clouds form.

Precipitation: rain, snow, hail returns water to land and sea.

Runoff: water flows into rivers → back to sea.

All living organisms need water — it's the solvent for all biochemical reactions.

The Nitrogen Cycle

Nitrogen makes up 78% of air — but plants can't use N₂ directly.

Nitrogen fixation: N₂ converted to ammonia/nitrates by nitrogen-fixing bacteria (in soil or root nodules of legumes).

Nitrification: ammonia converted to nitrates by nitrifying bacteria.

Denitrification: nitrates converted back to N₂ by denitrifying bacteria.

Decomposers: break down dead organisms → ammonia released (ammonification).

Biodiversity and Conservation

Biodiversity = variety of species in an area AND genetic diversity within species.

High biodiversity: more stable ecosystem — more connections, more resilience.

Threats to biodiversity: habitat destruction, pollution, invasive species, climate change, overexploitation.

Conservation: protecting habitats, captive breeding programmes, seed banks, legislation.

Benefits: ecosystem services (clean air/water, pollination, food), potential medicines, ethical responsibility.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Decomposers are fungi and bacteria — NOT worms or woodlice (these are detritivores that break material into smaller pieces but don't chemically decompose it). Both are important — but don't confuse the terms in exams.

📌 Key Note

Carbon cycle: photosynthesis removes CO₂; respiration, combustion, decomposition return CO₂. Fossil fuels are stored carbon.
