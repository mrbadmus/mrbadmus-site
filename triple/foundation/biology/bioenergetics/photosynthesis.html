🌿 Photosynthesis

Spec 4.4.1 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is Photosynthesis?

Plants use light energy to convert CO₂ and water into glucose and oxygen.

Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll which absorbs light (mainly red and blue, reflects green).

Photosynthesis is ENDOTHERMIC — light energy is stored as chemical energy in glucose.

Occurs in two stages: light reactions and the Calvin cycle (simplified at GCSE).

The Word and Symbol Equation

Carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen (using light energy).

6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Glucose is used for: respiration (energy), making cellulose (cell walls), making proteins, stored as starch.

Limiting Factors

If any one factor is in short supply, it limits the rate regardless of other conditions.

Light intensity: more light → faster rate (up to a limit).

CO₂ concentration: more CO₂ → faster rate (up to a limit).

Temperature: higher temp → faster rate (up to ~40°C — then enzymes denature).

At high light and CO₂, temperature is often the limiting factor.

Inverse Square Law and Light

Light intensity ∝ 1/distance².

Double the distance → ¼ the light intensity.

Used in experiments: moving a lamp further reduces light intensity predictably.

Greenhouses use artificial lighting and added CO₂ to maximise photosynthesis rate and crop yield.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Photosynthesis only happens in LIGHT. Respiration happens ALL THE TIME (day and night). In bright light, rate of photosynthesis > rate of respiration → net O₂ release. At the compensation point they are equal.

📐 Key Equations
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (light energy)
📌 Key Note

Limiting factors: light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature. Any one can be the bottleneck.
