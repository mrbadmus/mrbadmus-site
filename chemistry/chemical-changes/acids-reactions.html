⚡ Acids, Bases and Making Salts

Spec 4.4.1 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

The pH Scale

pH 0–6: acidic. pH 7: neutral. pH 8–14: alkaline.

Acids produce H⁺ ions in solution.

Alkalis produce OH⁻ ions in solution.

Neutralisation: H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O.

Strong acids fully dissociate (HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃).

Weak acids partially dissociate (ethanoic acid, citric acid).

Reactions of Acids

Acid + metal → salt + hydrogen.

Acid + metal oxide/hydroxide (base) → salt + water.

Acid + carbonate → salt + water + carbon dioxide.

Acid + alkali → salt + water (neutralisation).

Test H₂: burning splint → squeaky pop.

Test CO₂: limewater → turns cloudy.

Naming Salts

Hydrochloric acid → chloride salts (e.g. NaCl = sodium chloride).

Sulfuric acid → sulfate salts (e.g. CuSO₄ = copper sulfate).

Nitric acid → nitrate salts (e.g. KNO₃ = potassium nitrate).

Salt name = metal name + acid-derived name.

Making Pure Salt Crystals

Add excess solid (metal/carbonate/oxide) to acid — ensures all acid reacts.

Filter off excess solid.

Evaporate water gently → salt crystals form.

Dry the crystals.

For soluble salts: titration method — accurate volumes, no excess.

For insoluble salts: mix two soluble solutions → precipitate forms → filter and dry.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Acid + metal oxide gives salt + WATER (not hydrogen!). Hydrogen only comes from acid + reactive metal. Students confuse products all the time — learn each reaction type separately.

📐 Key Equations
Acid + metal → salt + H₂
Acid + base → salt + H₂O
Acid + carbonate → salt + H₂O + CO₂
H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O
📌 Key Note

Squeaky pop = H₂. Limewater cloudy = CO₂. Damp blue litmus turns red = acidic gas.
