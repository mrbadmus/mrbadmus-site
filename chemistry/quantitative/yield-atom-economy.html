⚡ Percentage Yield and Atom Economy

Spec 4.3.3 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

Percentage Yield

Theoretical yield = maximum mass you should get from the balanced equation.

Actual yield = what you actually collect in the lab.

Yield is always less than 100% — some is always lost.

Reasons: reversible reactions, product lost in filtering/transferring, impurities, side reactions.

Atom Economy

Atom economy = what fraction of reactant atoms end up in the DESIRED product.

Calculated from the balanced equation using Mr values — not actual masses.

100% atom economy = no waste at all (all atoms go into useful product).

Industry prefers high atom economy → less waste, lower cost, more sustainable.

Green Chemistry

Green chemistry aims to make reactions more sustainable.

High atom economy, renewable feedstocks, catalysts (lower energy), room temp reactions.

Haber process has 100% atom economy (N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃ — only one product).

But only ~15% yield per pass — recycled gases improve overall efficiency.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Atom economy uses Mr from the EQUATION — not actual experimental masses. Percentage yield uses ACTUAL masses from the experiment. These are two completely different calculations.

📐 Key Equations
% yield = (actual yield ÷ theoretical yield) × 100
Atom economy = (Mr of desired product ÷ sum of Mr of ALL products) × 100
