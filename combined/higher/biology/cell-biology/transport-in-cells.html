🌿 Diffusion, Osmosis and Active Transport

Spec 4.1.3 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

Diffusion

Diffusion = net movement of particles from HIGH concentration to LOW concentration.

Down the concentration gradient — no energy required (passive).

Faster if: higher concentration gradient, higher temperature, shorter distance, larger surface area.

Examples: O₂ into cells, CO₂ out of cells, glucose into cells from gut.

Osmosis

Osmosis = diffusion of WATER molecules across a partially permeable membrane.

Water moves from dilute solution (high water potential) to concentrated solution (low water potential).

Down the water potential gradient — passive, no energy needed.

Plant cells in dilute solution → swell → turgor (firm).

Plant cells in concentrated solution → shrink → plasmolysis.

Active Transport

Movement of substances from LOW to HIGH concentration — against the gradient.

Requires ENERGY (ATP from respiration) and carrier proteins.

Examples: mineral ions from soil into root hair cells; glucose from gut into blood.

If respiration is inhibited, active transport stops — unlike diffusion and osmosis.

Exchange Surfaces

Efficient exchange surfaces have:

Large surface area (many folds/villi).

Thin membrane (short diffusion distance).

Concentration gradient maintained (good blood supply, ventilation).

Examples: alveoli in lungs, villi in small intestine, root hair cells in plants.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Osmosis is a SPECIAL TYPE of diffusion — only water molecules, across a partially permeable membrane. Active transport goes AGAINST the gradient and needs ENERGY. Diffusion and osmosis are both passive (no energy needed).

📌 Key Note

Diffusion and osmosis: passive (no ATP). Active transport: needs ATP from respiration.
