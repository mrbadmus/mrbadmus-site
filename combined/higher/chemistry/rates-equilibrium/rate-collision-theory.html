🧪 Rate of Reaction and Collision Theory

Spec 4.6.1 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

Rate of Reaction

Rate = how quickly reactants become products.

Measured as: amount of product formed per unit time, or amount of reactant used per unit time.

Faster rate = steeper graph at the start.

Rate = quantity ÷ time (e.g. g/s, cm³/s).

Collision Theory

Reactions happen when particles collide with SUFFICIENT ENERGY.

Activation energy = minimum energy needed for a successful collision.

More frequent collisions + more energetic collisions = faster rate.

Any factor increasing collision frequency or energy speeds up the reaction.

Factors Affecting Rate

Temperature ↑ → particles move faster → more collisions AND more exceed activation energy → big rate increase.

Concentration ↑ → more particles per volume → more frequent collisions → faster rate.

Surface area ↑ → more exposed reactant surface → more contact → faster rate.

Catalyst → lowers activation energy → more particles can react → faster rate.

Catalysts

Speed up reactions without being consumed.

Provide an alternative pathway with lower activation energy.

Enzymes = biological catalysts (proteins).

Industrial: iron (Haber), platinum (catalytic converter), vanadium pentoxide (Contact process).

Catalysts allow lower operating temperatures → saves energy → cheaper.

⚠️ Common Mistake

A catalyst is NOT used up — it's unchanged at the end. Also, temperature affects rate much MORE dramatically than concentration because it affects BOTH collision frequency AND the proportion of particles above activation energy.

Rate = quantity ÷ time
