Concentration of Solutions

Spec 5.3.2.5 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is Concentration?

The CONCENTRATION of a solution tells us how much solute is dissolved per unit volume of solution.
A CONCENTRATED solution has a LOT of solute dissolved in a given volume.
A DILUTE solution has a LITTLE solute dissolved in the same volume.
Concentration can be expressed in two ways at Foundation level:
1. In g/dm³ (grams per decimetre cubed):
Concentration (g/dm³) = mass of solute (g) ÷ volume of solution (dm³)
2. In g/cm³ (grams per centimetre cubed) — less common:
Concentration (g/cm³) = mass of solute (g) ÷ volume of solution (cm³)
Note: 1 dm³ = 1000 cm³ = 1 litre

Calculating Concentration

FORMULA:
concentration (g/dm³) = mass of solute (g) ÷ volume of solution (dm³)
Rearranging:
mass (g) = concentration (g/dm³) × volume (dm³)
volume (dm³) = mass (g) ÷ concentration (g/dm³)
IMPORTANT: volume must be in dm³. If given in cm³, divide by 1000 first.
250 cm³ = 0.250 dm³
500 cm³ = 0.500 dm³
EXAMPLES:
1. 10 g of NaCl dissolved in 0.5 dm³ (500 cm³) of water.
Concentration = 10 ÷ 0.5 = 20 g/dm³
2. A solution has concentration 50 g/dm³. What mass is in 200 cm³?
Volume = 200 ÷ 1000 = 0.2 dm³
Mass = 50 × 0.2 = 10 g
3. 8 g of glucose gives a concentration of 16 g/dm³. What volume was used?
Volume = 8 ÷ 16 = 0.5 dm³ = 500 cm³

Dilution

DILUTION means adding more solvent (usually water) to reduce the concentration of a solution.
When you dilute a solution:
The AMOUNT of solute stays the SAME.
The VOLUME increases.
The CONCENTRATION decreases.
Dilution formula:
concentration₁ × volume₁ = concentration₂ × volume₂
Example:
100 cm³ of 40 g/dm³ solution is diluted to 400 cm³. What is the new concentration?
c₁V₁ = c₂V₂
40 × 0.1 = c₂ × 0.4
4 = c₂ × 0.4
c₂ = 4 ÷ 0.4 = 10 g/dm³
SERIAL DILUTION:
A technique where a solution is diluted by a fixed factor multiple times.
Useful for making a range of known concentrations.
Example: Start with 100 g/dm³ → dilute 1:10 → 10 g/dm³ → dilute 1:10 → 1 g/dm³.
⚠️ Common Mistake

Volume MUST be in dm³ when using the concentration formula. If given in cm³, divide by 1000 first. 250 cm³ = 0.250 dm³. Students frequently forget this conversion and get answers 1000 times too large or too small.

📐 Variables
cConcentration (c) is measured in g/dm³ (g/dm³)
mMass of solute (m) is measured in grams (g)
VVolume of solution (V) is measured in dm³ (dm³)
📐 Key Equations
concentration (g/dm³) = mass (g) ÷ volume (dm³)
mass (g) = concentration (g/dm³) × volume (dm³)
volume (dm³) = mass (g) ÷ concentration (g/dm³)
📌 Key Note

Concentration (g/dm³) = mass (g) ÷ volume (dm³). Convert cm³ to dm³: ÷ 1000. Concentrated = lots of solute per volume. Dilute = little solute per volume. Dilution: same mass, larger volume, lower concentration. c₁V₁ = c₂V₂.

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Convert between g/dm³ and mol/dm³ using molar mass. c (mol/dm³) = mass (g) ÷ (Mr × V (dm³)). Use titration data to calculate unknown concentrations. Understand that mol/dm³ is the standard unit in Higher-level calculations.
