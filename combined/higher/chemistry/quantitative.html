🧪 Quantitative Chemistry

Spec 4.3 Paper 1 📙 Higher
📋 What You Need to Know
  • ● Relative atomic mass (Ar) and relative formula mass (Mr)
  • ● Moles — mol = mass ÷ Mr
  • ● Concentration — mol = concentration × volume
  • ● Gas volumes — mol = volume ÷ 24 (at RTP)
  • ● Percentage yield
  • ● Atom economy
  • ● Balancing equations
📐 Key Equations
mol = mass ÷ Mr
mol = c × V
mol = V ÷ 24
% yield = actual ÷ theoretical × 100
Atom economy = Mr useful ÷ Mr all × 100

🎯 Quick Quiz
1. Calculate the Mr of H₂O. (Ar: H=1, O=16)
Correct! Mr = 2(1) + 16 = 18 ✅
Not quite. Mr of H₂O = 2×1 + 16 = 18
2. How many moles are in 56 g of iron? (Ar of Fe = 56)
Correct! mol = 56 ÷ 56 = 1 mol ✅
Not quite. mol = mass ÷ Mr = 56 ÷ 56 = 1 mol
3. A reaction produces 8 g of product but the theoretical yield is 10 g. What is the percentage yield?
Correct! % yield = (8 ÷ 10) × 100 = 80% ✅
Not quite. % yield = (actual ÷ theoretical) × 100 = (8 ÷ 10) × 100 = 80%
