Structure and Properties of Ionic Compounds

Spec 5.2.2.3 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

Reviewing the Giant Ionic Lattice

Ionic compounds have a GIANT IONIC LATTICE structure:
Regular 3D arrangement of alternating positive and negative ions.
Strong electrostatic forces acting in ALL DIRECTIONS between oppositely charged ions.
This structure extends throughout the entire crystal — billions of ions held together.
The FORMULA of an ionic compound shows the SIMPLEST RATIO of ions — not the actual number in a formula unit.
NaCl: 1 Na⁺ for every 1 Cl⁻.
MgO: 1 Mg²⁺ for every 1 O²⁻.
CaCl₂: 1 Ca²⁺ for every 2 Cl⁻.
The lattice structure explains ALL properties.

Full Property Explanation

HIGH MELTING AND BOILING POINTS:
Many strong electrostatic bonds throughout the lattice.
Very large amounts of energy needed to separate the ions.
Example: NaCl melts at 801°C. MgO (higher charged ions) melts at 2852°C.
Higher ionic charges → stronger forces → higher melting point.
BRITTLENESS:
Ionic compounds are HARD but BRITTLE.
When a force is applied, layers of ions shift by one position.
Like-charged ions end up aligned → strong REPULSION → crystal cracks/shatters.
Cannot bend without breaking — unlike metals.
ELECTRICAL CONDUCTIVITY:
SOLID: Does NOT conduct — ions are in fixed positions in the lattice, cannot move.
MOLTEN (liquid): DOES conduct — ions are free to move and carry charge.
DISSOLVED in water: DOES conduct — ions separate and can move through the solution.
SOLUBILITY:
Many ionic compounds dissolve in WATER — polar water molecules attract and surround the ions, pulling them from the lattice.
Not all are soluble — depends on the strength of ion-ion forces vs. ion-water forces.

Comparing Ionic Compounds — Effect of Charge

The CHARGE on the ions affects the strength of the electrostatic forces:
Higher charge → stronger attraction → higher melting point → higher electrical conductivity when molten.
Comparison:
NaCl (Na⁺ and Cl⁻ — charges of ±1): melting point 801°C.
MgO (Mg²⁺ and O²⁻ — charges of ±2): melting point 2852°C.
MgO has MUCH stronger ionic bonds because both ions have double the charge — the electrostatic force is proportional to the product of the charges.
This also means more energy is released when MgO forms — MgO is a more stable compound.
This concept helps explain why Group 2 compounds often have higher melting points than Group 1 compounds.
⚠️ Common Mistake

Ionic compounds conduct in solution because the IONS move — NOT because electrons move. This is IONIC conduction (different from ELECTRONIC conduction in metals). Also: ionic compounds are brittle, not ductile. They crack when struck because shifting layers brings like charges together → repulsion → shattering.

📌 Key Note

Giant ionic lattice → high MP/BP (strong electrostatic forces). Brittle (layer shift → repulsion → shatters). Solid: no conduction. Molten/dissolved: conducts (mobile ions). Higher ionic charge → stronger forces → higher MP. Many dissolve in water.
