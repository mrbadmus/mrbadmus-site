⚡ Energy Stores and Systems

Spec 4.1.1 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is an energy store?

Energy is always stored somewhere before it moves.
Think of energy stores like different types of bank accounts — each holds energy in a different way.
Energy can never be created or destroyed — it just moves between stores.

The 8 Energy Stores

Kinetic — energy of moving objects (a rolling ball, a speeding car).
Gravitational potential — energy due to height (a book on a shelf, a skydiver before jumping).
Elastic potential — energy stored in a stretched or squashed object (a spring, a rubber band).
Chemical — energy stored in bonds (food, fuel, batteries).
Thermal — energy stored due to temperature (a hot cup of tea).
Nuclear — energy stored in the nucleus of atoms (used in power stations).
Electromagnetic — energy carried by light and radiation.
Electrostatic — energy stored between charged objects.

How energy transfers between stores

Energy moves between stores by four pathways:
Mechanically — a force moving an object (pushing, pulling).
Electrically — charge moving through a circuit.
By heating — thermal energy flowing from hot to cold.
By radiation — light or sound waves carrying energy.

A real example — the bouncing ball

Ball held still at height → gravitational potential store is full.
Ball drops → gravitational potential transfers to kinetic store.
Ball hits floor → kinetic transfers to elastic potential (squash) then back to kinetic.
Ball rises → kinetic transfers back to gravitational potential.
Each bounce loses a little to thermal store (heat) — that's why it doesn't bounce as high!
⚠️ Common Mistake

Students say 'energy is lost' — energy is NEVER lost. It's transferred to the thermal store (heat) and spreads out into the surroundings. Always say 'energy is dissipated as heat' not 'energy is lost'.
