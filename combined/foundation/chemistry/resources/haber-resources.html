🧪 The Haber Process, Fertilisers and Using Resources

Spec 4.10 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Haber Process

Reaction: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (exothermic forward reaction).
Raw materials: N₂ from fractional distillation of air; H₂ from natural gas + steam.
Conditions: ~450°C, ~200 atm, iron catalyst.
Yield: ~15% per pass — unreacted gases are recycled.

NPK Fertilisers

Plants need N (nitrogen for protein/growth), P (phosphorus for DNA/roots), K (potassium for enzymes).
NPK fertilisers contain compounds supplying all three.
Excessive use causes eutrophication: fertilisers wash into rivers → algal bloom → O₂ depleted → fish die.
Ammonia → nitric acid + ammonia → ammonium nitrate (N-rich fertiliser).

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)

LCA evaluates the environmental impact of a product throughout its entire life.
Stages: obtaining raw materials → manufacturing → use → disposal.
All stages considered: energy use, water use, waste produced, CO₂ emissions.
Helps compare products — e.g. is a reusable bag better than disposable?

Water Treatment

Drinking water must be safe.
Steps: sedimentation → filtration (sand/gravel) → chlorination (kills bacteria).
Distilled water (pure): used in labs — but too expensive for drinking water at scale.
Fluoridation: some areas add fluoride to prevent tooth decay (controversial).
⚠️ Common Mistake

Higher temperature reduces ammonia yield (exothermic forward reaction disfavoured at high temp). Lower temperature increases yield but slows rate. 450°C is the compromise.
