Chemistry · Bonding, Structure and Properties of Matter

States of Matter and State Symbols

Spec 5.2.2.1–5.2.2.2 📗 Foundation
📖 Teach me 🎮 Labs 🎯 Test me
Theory · read, then commit

All matter exists in one of three states — solid, liquid or gas. The difference is how the particles are arranged and how freely they move.

🧊Solid
ArrangementRegular, closely packed
MovementVibrate about fixed positions
Shape & volumeFixed shape, fixed volume
Compressible?No
💧Liquid
ArrangementClose but random
MovementFlow past each other
Shape & volumeTakes container shape, fixed volume
Compressible?No
💨Gas
ArrangementFar apart, spread out
MovementFast, random, all directions
Shape & volumeFills any container
Compressible?Yes
How freely the particles move drives everything else — shape, volume and whether it can be compressed.
Checkpoint · quick check
Describe the arrangement and movement of the particles in a solid.
🫠Melting / Freezing
Solid ⇌ liquid. Melting adds energy; freezing removes it.
♨️Boiling / Condensing
Liquid ⇌ gas. Boiling adds energy; condensing removes it.
❄️Subliming
Some solids go straight to gas (iodine, dry ice). All these are physical changes.
Checkpoint · quick check
Name the change of state that occurs when a liquid turns into a gas.
🔤State symbols in equations
(s) solid · (l) liquid · (g) gas · (aq) dissolved in water.
2H₂(g) + O₂(g) → 2H₂O(l)
NaCl(s) → Na⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq)

⭐Examiner Tip
Melting and boiling are physical changes — the particles rearrange but the substance is unchanged (ice, water and steam are all H₂O), so don't call them chemical changes. And use the state symbol (aq) only for something dissolved in water, not for any liquid.

Key note · cover it, say it, check it
Revision card

Solid: fixed positions, vibrate. Liquid: close but can flow. Gas: widely spread, rapid random motion. Melting (s→l), boiling (l→g), condensing (g→l), freezing (l→s). State symbols: (s) solid, (l) liquid, (g) gas, (aq) aqueous solution.
