⚡ Energy Transfers in Electrical Circuits

Spec 4.2.4 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

Energy and charge

E = Q × V — the energy transferred equals the charge that flows multiplied by the potential difference. This links the charge equation (Q = It) with energy. Substituting: E = I × t × V = P × t. All three equations are connected.

Paying for electricity — kilowatt-hours

In everyday life, energy is measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh). 1 kWh = the energy used by a 1 kW device in 1 hour = 3,600,000 J = 3.6 MJ. Your electricity meter measures kWh. Cost = power (kW) × time (hours) × price per kWh.

Transformers and the National Grid

Step-UP transformer: increases voltage, decreases current (for efficient long-distance transmission). Step-DOWN transformer: decreases voltage, increases current (for safe home use). Transformer equation: Vp/Vs = Np/Ns (covered in Magnetism topic).

⭐ Higher Tier

For 100% efficient transformer: Vp × Ip = Vs × Is (power in = power out). Real transformers are very close to 100% efficient — energy losses are mainly due to resistance heating in the coils.

🎯 Test Yourself
1. A 2 kW heater runs for 3 hours. How many kWh does it use?
Correct! Energy = 2 kW × 3 h = 6 kWh ✅
Not quite. Energy (kWh) = power (kW) × time (h) = 2 × 3 = 6 kWh.
2. What does a step-up transformer do?
Correct! Step-up: ↑ voltage, ↓ current — used in the National Grid to reduce transmission losses ✅
Not quite. Step-up transformer increases voltage AND decreases current (power = VI stays constant for 100% efficiency).
3. How many joules are in 1 kWh?
Correct! 1 kWh = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3,600,000 J ✅
Not quite. 1 kWh = 1 kW × 1 hour = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3,600,000 J.
