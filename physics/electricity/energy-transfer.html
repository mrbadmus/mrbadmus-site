⚡ Energy Transfers in Electrical Circuits

Spec 4.2.4 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

E = Pt — Energy from Power and Time

Energy = Power × Time.

Think: a powerful appliance running for a long time transfers a LOT of energy.

Time MUST be in seconds for the answer to be in Joules.

If you want the answer in kWh (for electricity bills), keep power in kW and time in hours.

E = QV — Energy from Charge and P.D.

Each unit of charge carries energy equal to the p.d. it moves through.

So total energy = total charge × potential difference.

This links nicely with Q = It — substituting gives E = I × t × V = P × t. All connected!

Paying for Electricity — kWh

Your electricity meter counts energy in kilowatt-hours (kWh).

1 kWh = the energy used by a 1 kW appliance in 1 hour.

1 kWh = 1000 × 3600 = 3,600,000 J = 3.6 MJ.

Cost = power (kW) × time (hours) × price per kWh.

Transformers in the National Grid

Step-UP transformer: increases p.d., decreases current — used to send electricity across the country.

Step-DOWN transformer: decreases p.d., increases current — used before electricity enters homes.

For a 100% efficient transformer: power in = power out, so Vp × Ip = Vs × Is.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Mixing up E = Pt (energy from power) with P = VI (power from voltage and current). They're related but different! Also — when using kWh for electricity bills, keep power in kW and time in HOURS. Switch to seconds only when you need Joules.

📐 Variables
EEnergy (E) is measured in Joules (J)
PPower (P) is measured in Watts (W)
tTime (t) is measured in Seconds (s)
QCharge (Q) is measured in Coulombs (C)
VPotential Difference (V) is measured in Volts (V)

Example 1 — Using E = QV

A charge of 500 C flows through a device with a p.d. of 12 V across it. Calculate the energy transferred.

F

E = Q × V

I

E = 500 × 12

F

No unit conversion needed

A

E = 6000 J

Example 2 — Using E = Pt (with time conversion)

A 1500 W electric heater runs for 2 hours. Calculate the energy transferred in Joules and in kWh.

F

In Joules: E = P × t (t in seconds). In kWh: E = P(kW) × t(hours)

I

In Joules: t = 2 × 3600 = 7200 s → E = 1500 × 7200. In kWh: P = 1.5 kW, t = 2 h → E = 1.5 × 2

F

Two different approaches — both valid

A

E = 10,800,000 J = 10.8 MJ. In kWh: E = 3 kWh

Example 3 — Electricity bill calculation

A 2.5 kW tumble dryer runs for 90 minutes. Electricity costs 28p per kWh. Calculate the cost.

F

Energy (kWh) = P(kW) × t(hours). Cost = energy × price per kWh

I

t = 90 min = 1.5 hours. Energy = 2.5 × 1.5 = 3.75 kWh

F

Cost = 3.75 × 28p

A

Cost = 105p = £1.05
