⚡ Series and Parallel Circuits

Spec 4.2.2 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

Series circuit rules

1. CURRENT: same everywhere — I is identical at every point in a series circuit. 2. POTENTIAL DIFFERENCE: shared between components — V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃. 3. RESISTANCE: adds up — R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃. Adding more components in series increases total resistance and decreases current.

Parallel circuit rules

1. POTENTIAL DIFFERENCE: same across every branch — V is identical across every parallel branch. 2. CURRENT: splits between branches — I_total = I₁ + I₂ + I₃. 3. RESISTANCE: total resistance is LESS than the smallest individual branch resistance. Adding more branches in parallel decreases total resistance and increases total current.

Why parallel is used in homes

All appliances in your home are connected in parallel. This means: each device gets the full 230 V. Turning one device off doesn't affect others. Each device can be controlled independently with its own switch.

Calculating current in parallel branches

Each branch: use I = V ÷ R (same V across all branches). Total current from battery = sum of all branch currents. Total resistance: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ (Higher Tier).

📐 Key Equations
R_total = R₁ + R₂ (series)
V_total = V₁ + V₂ (series)
1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ (parallel, Higher)
⚽ FIFA Worked Example
⚽ FIFA Worked Example

Two resistors of 4 Ω and 8 Ω are connected in series to a 24 V battery. Calculate (a) total resistance, (b) current, (c) p.d. across each resistor.

F

(a) R_total = R₁ + R₂. (b) I = V ÷ R_total. (c) V = I × R for each

I

(a) R_total = 4 + 8 = 12 Ω. (b) I = 24 ÷ 12 = 2 A. (c) V₁ = 2×4 = 8 V, V₂ = 2×8 = 16 V

F

Check: 8 + 16 = 24 V ✅ (p.d.s must add up to battery p.d.)

A

R_total = 12 Ω, I = 2 A, V₁ = 8 V, V₂ = 16 V

⭐ Higher Tier

Calculating total resistance in parallel: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ... For two resistors: R_total = (R₁ × R₂) ÷ (R₁ + R₂). The total is always less than the smallest resistor.

1. In a series circuit, what is the same at every point?
Correct! In series, current is the same everywhere ✅
Not quite. In series: current is the same everywhere. P.d. splits. In parallel: p.d. is the same across each branch.
2. Two 6 Ω resistors are in series with a 12 V battery. What is the current?
Correct! R_total = 12 Ω, I = V ÷ R = 12 ÷ 12 = 1 A ✅
Not quite. R_total = 6 + 6 = 12 Ω. I = V ÷ R = 12 ÷ 12 = 1 A.
3. Why are household appliances connected in parallel?
Correct! Parallel means full 230 V to each appliance and independent switching ✅
Not quite. Parallel gives each appliance the full p.d. (230 V) and allows them to work independently.
