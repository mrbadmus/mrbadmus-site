⚡ Charge, Current and Time

Spec 4.2.1.2 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is electric current?

Electric current is the rate of flow of charge. In metal wires, it's actually electrons moving. Current is measured in amperes (A) using an ammeter connected in series.

What is charge?

Electric charge is measured in coulombs (C). One coulomb is the charge carried by approximately 6.24 × 10¹⁸ electrons. The charge on one electron is −1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C.

The equation: Q = It

Q = charge in coulombs (C). I = current in amperes (A). t = time in SECONDS (s) — always convert minutes to seconds! Rearranged: I = Q ÷ t and t = Q ÷ I.

Common mistake — time units!

Always convert time to seconds before using Q = It. 1 minute = 60 seconds. 1 hour = 3600 seconds. If you leave time in minutes, your answer will be wrong by a factor of 60!
