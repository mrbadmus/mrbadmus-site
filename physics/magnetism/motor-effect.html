⚡ The Motor Effect and Fleming's Left Hand Rule

Spec 4.7.2 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Motor Effect

A current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a force.

This is because the current creates its own magnetic field → interacts with the external field → produces a force.

This is the principle behind every electric motor.

Fleming's Left Hand Rule

Hold your left hand with:

First finger pointing in direction of magnetic Field (N to S).

Second finger pointing in direction of conventional Current (+ to -).

Thumb points in direction of thrust (Motion/Force).

The three are mutually perpendicular — all at 90° to each other.

If any two are reversed, the force reverses too.

F = BIL — The Motor Force Equation

F = force in Newtons (N).

B = magnetic flux density (field strength) in Tesla (T).

I = current in Amperes (A).

L = length of conductor in the field in metres (m).

Double any one = double the force. Halve any one = halve the force.

The Electric Motor

A coil of wire carrying current sits in a magnetic field.

Opposite sides of the coil experience forces in opposite directions → rotation.

The commutator (split ring) reverses the current every half turn → keeps rotation in the same direction.

DC motor: powered by direct current.

Speed increased by: more current, stronger magnets, more turns on coil.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Fleming's Left Hand Rule is for MOTORS (current → force). Fleming's RIGHT Hand Rule is for GENERATORS (motion → current/voltage). Don't mix them up — left for motor effect, right for generators.

⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Example 1 — Motor force

A wire of length 0.3 m carries a current of 4 A in a magnetic field of 0.5 T. Calculate the force on the wire.

F

F = B × I × L

I

F = 0.5 × 4 × 0.3

F

No unit conversion needed

A

F = 0.6 N

Example 2 — Find current

A 0.2 m wire in a 0.8 T field experiences a force of 0.4 N. Calculate the current.

F

I = F ÷ (B × L)

I

I = 0.4 ÷ (0.8 × 0.2) = 0.4 ÷ 0.16

F

No unit conversion needed

A

I = 2.5 A

⭐ Higher Tier Only

A current at right angles to the field gives maximum force. If current is parallel to field, force = zero. For angles between: F = BIL sin(θ).
