⚡ The Nervous System and Homeostasis

Spec 4.5 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is Homeostasis?

Homeostasis = maintaining a constant internal environment.

Controlled conditions: body temperature (37°C), blood glucose levels, water balance.

Negative feedback: if a variable goes above/below the set point, the system responds to bring it back.

Involves: receptor (detects change), coordination centre (processes signal), effector (muscle or gland responds).

The Nervous System

Central nervous system (CNS): brain + spinal cord — processes information.

Peripheral nervous system: sensory and motor neurones connecting CNS to body.

Sensory neurone: carries signal FROM receptor TO CNS.

Motor neurone: carries signal FROM CNS TO effector (muscle/gland).

Relay neurone: connects sensory and motor neurones in the CNS.

Synapses

Gap between neurones — signals can't jump across electrically.

Neurotransmitters released from one neurone → diffuse across gap → bind to receptors on next neurone.

One-way transmission — neurotransmitters only released in one direction.

Drugs can affect synapses: stimulants increase neurotransmitter release; sedatives block receptors.

The Reflex Arc

Reflex = fast, automatic response to stimulus — bypasses conscious brain.

Pathway: stimulus → receptor → sensory neurone → relay neurone (in spinal cord) → motor neurone → effector (muscle/gland).

Faster than a voluntary response — protects from harm.

Examples: knee-jerk reflex, pupil response to light, withdrawing hand from hot object.

⚠️ Common Mistake

A reflex arc goes through the SPINAL CORD (or brainstem), not the thinking brain. That's why reflexes are faster — they don't wait for conscious processing. Students often say 'the brain controls reflexes' — it doesn't (except for certain brainstem reflexes).

📌 Key Note

Reflex order: stimulus → receptor → sensory neurone → relay neurone → motor neurone → effector → response.
