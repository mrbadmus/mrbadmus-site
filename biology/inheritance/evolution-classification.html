⚡ Evolution, Natural Selection and Classification

Spec 4.6.4 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

Darwin's Theory of Natural Selection

1. All organisms show variation (differences in characteristics).

2. All populations overproduce offspring — struggle for survival.

3. Some variants are better adapted to the environment — greater chance of survival.

4. Better-adapted individuals survive and reproduce — passing on favourable alleles.

5. Over many generations, advantageous traits become more common in the population.

Evidence for Evolution

Fossil record: shows gradual changes in organisms over time.

Antibiotic resistance: bacteria evolve resistance rapidly — direct observation of natural selection.

Homologous structures: similar bone structure in different organisms (e.g. human arm, whale flipper, bat wing) suggest common ancestor.

DNA comparisons: closely related species have more similar DNA sequences.

Classification

Traditional classification: based on physical characteristics (Linnaeus, 1700s).

Modern classification: based on evolutionary relationships and DNA evidence.

The five kingdoms (simplified): animals, plants, fungi, prokaryotes, protists.

Binomial nomenclature: two-part Latin name — genus + species (e.g. Homo sapiens).

Phylogenetic trees show evolutionary relationships between species.

Selective Breeding and Genetic Engineering

Selective breeding: humans choose organisms with desired traits to breed together.

Over generations: traits enhanced (e.g. large wheat seeds, docile cattle, fast racehorses).

Genetic engineering: inserting a specific gene into an organism's DNA.

Examples: insulin-producing bacteria (human insulin gene inserted), GM crops (herbicide resistant, higher yield).

Both have ethical considerations — reducing genetic diversity, unknown long-term effects.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Natural selection acts on VARIATION that already EXISTS in a population — it does NOT cause mutations. Evolution is the change in allele frequency over many generations. Individuals don't evolve; populations do.

📌 Key Note

Natural selection: variation → selection pressure → survival of the fittest → reproduction → allele frequency changes over generations.
