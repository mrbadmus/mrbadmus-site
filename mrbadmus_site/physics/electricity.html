● Charge, Current and Time 4.2.1.2

Electric current is the rate of flow of charge. In metal wires, it's actually electrons moving. Current is measured in amperes (A) using an ammeter connected in series.

Q = ItI = Q ÷ tt = Q ÷ I

● Potential Difference, Current and Resistance 4.2.1.3 📋 RP

Potential difference (never call it 'voltage' in physics!) is the energy transferred per unit charge between two points in a circuit. It is the 'push' that drives current around. Measured in volts (V) using a voltmeter in PARALLEL.

V = IRI = V ÷ RR = V ÷ I

● Resistance — Components and Their Characteristics 4.2.1.4 ⭐ Higher📋 RP

Resistance stays constant as long as temperature stays constant. I-V graph: straight line through origin. Examples: metal wire at constant temp, fixed resistors in circuits.

● Series and Parallel Circuits 4.2.2 ⭐ Higher

1. CURRENT: same everywhere — I is identical at every point in a series circuit. 2. POTENTIAL DIFFERENCE: shared between components — V_total = V₁ + V₂ + V₃. 3. RESISTANCE: adds up — R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃. Adding more components in series increases total resistance and decreases current.

R_total = R₁ + R₂ (series)V_total = V₁ + V₂ (series)1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ (parallel, Higher)

● Mains Electricity — AC, DC and Wiring 4.2.3

DC (Direct Current): current always flows in the same direction. Source: batteries and cells. AC (Alternating Current): current repeatedly reverses direction. Source: UK mains, generators. UK mains: 230 V, 50 Hz (changes direction 50 times per second).

UK mains: 230 V, 50 Hz

● Electrical Power 4.2.4 ⭐ Higher

Power is the rate of energy transfer — how quickly a device uses energy. Measured in watts (W). 1 watt = 1 joule per second. A 2000 W kettle transfers 2000 J every second.

P = VIP = I²RE = PtE = QV

● Energy Transfers in Electrical Circuits 4.2.4 ⭐ Higher

E = Q × V — the energy transferred equals the charge that flows multiplied by the potential difference. This links the charge equation (Q = It) with energy. Substituting: E = I × t × V = P × t. All three equations are connected.

E = QVE = Pt1 kWh = 3,600,000 J

● Static Electricity 4.2.5 ⭐ Higher🔬 Triple

When two insulators are rubbed together, electrons transfer from one to the other. The object that gains electrons becomes negatively charged. The object that loses electrons becomes positively charged. Only electrons move — protons stay in the nucleus.

F = EQ (Higher)E = V/d (Higher)

🎯 Quick Quiz
1. A charge of 30 C flows past a point in 6 seconds. What is the current?
Correct! I = Q ÷ t = 30 ÷ 6 = 5 A ✅
Not quite. Use I = Q ÷ t → I = 30 ÷ 6 = 5 A
2. Which component has resistance that DECREASES as temperature increases?
Correct! NTC thermistor: ↑ temperature → ↓ resistance ✅
Not quite. A thermistor (NTC) decreases in resistance as temperature rises.
3. In a parallel circuit, what is the same across every branch?
Correct! P.D. is the same across all parallel branches ✅
Not quite. In parallel: p.d. is the same across every branch. Current splits.
