⚡ Momentum

Spec 4.5.7 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is Momentum?

Momentum is a measure of how hard it is to stop a moving object.

A slow, heavy lorry and a fast, light bullet can have the same momentum.

Momentum is a vector — it has direction as well as size.

Objects moving in opposite directions have momenta in opposite signs.

Conservation of Momentum

In a closed system (no external forces), total momentum before = total momentum after.

This applies in ALL collisions and explosions.

When two cars collide: momentum before collision = momentum after.

When a gun is fired: bullet goes forward, gun recoils backward — total momentum stays zero.

Newton's 2nd Law — The Momentum Version

Force = rate of change of momentum = (mv − mu) ÷ t.

A larger force causes a greater rate of change of momentum.

This is why airbags save lives — they increase the time of impact, reducing the force on the passenger.

Same change in momentum, longer time = smaller force.

Crumple Zones and Safety

Crumple zones increase the time taken for a car to stop in a crash.

Longer stopping time → smaller force (same impulse spread over more time).

Airbags, seatbelts and helmets all work on the same principle.

Impulse = Force × time = change in momentum.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Momentum is a VECTOR — direction matters. If two objects move in opposite directions, one has positive momentum and one has negative. Always define a positive direction at the start of your calculation.

Example 2 — Conservation of momentum (collision)

Car A (mass 1000 kg, velocity 15 m/s) collides with stationary Car B (mass 1500 kg). They stick together. Find their combined velocity after the collision.

F

Total momentum before = total momentum after. p_before = m_A × v_A + m_B × 0

I

p_before = 1000 × 15 = 15,000 kg m/s. After: (m_A + m_B) × v = 15,000

F

v = 15,000 ÷ (1000 + 1500) = 15,000 ÷ 2500

A

v = 6 m/s

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Impulse = force × time = change in momentum (Δmv). Impulse is also a vector. Units: N·s = kg·m/s.
