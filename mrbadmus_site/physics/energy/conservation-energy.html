⚡ Conservation and Dissipation of Energy

Spec 4.1.2 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Law of Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed.

It can only be transferred from one store to another.

The total energy in a closed system always stays the same.

This is one of the most fundamental laws in all of science — nothing breaks it.

Useful and Wasted Energy

In any energy transfer, some energy goes where we want it (useful) and some spreads out (wasted).

Wasted energy usually ends up as thermal energy in the surroundings.

Example: a light bulb transfers electrical energy → light (useful) + heat (wasted).

The wasted energy isn't gone — it's just spread out and no longer useful.

Efficiency

Efficiency tells us what fraction of energy is actually useful.

A 100% efficient machine puts ALL its energy into the useful output — impossible in reality!

A 75% efficient motor wastes 25% as heat.

The closer to 1 (or 100%), the better.

Reducing Energy Waste

Lubrication reduces friction between moving parts — less heat wasted.

Insulation reduces heat loss from homes and appliances.

Streamlining reduces air resistance — less energy wasted overcoming drag.

LED bulbs are more efficient than old filament bulbs — less heat wasted.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Efficiency has NO UNITS if expressed as a decimal (0.75) or as a percentage (75%). Students often write 'J' or 'W' after efficiency — don't! Also — efficiency can NEVER be greater than 1 (or 100%). If your answer is over 1, you've made an error.

📐 Variables
efficiencyEfficiency (efficiency) is measured in no unit (or %) ()
useful outputUseful energy output (useful output) is measured in Joules (J)
total inputTotal energy input (total input) is measured in Joules (J)
📐 Key Equations
Efficiency = useful output energy ÷ total input energy
📌 Key Note

To convert efficiency to percentage: multiply by 100. E.g. efficiency = 0.6 → 60%

⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Example 1 — Calculate efficiency

A motor takes in 500 J of electrical energy and produces 350 J of useful kinetic energy. Calculate the efficiency.

F

Efficiency = useful output ÷ total input

I

Efficiency = 350 ÷ 500

F

No unit conversion needed

A

Efficiency = 0.7 = 70%

Example 2 — Find wasted energy

A 200 W bulb is 15% efficient as a light source. How much power is wasted as heat?

F

Useful power = efficiency × total input. Wasted = total − useful

I

Useful = 0.15 × 200 = 30 W. Wasted = 200 − 30

F

No conversion needed

A

Wasted = 170 W as heat

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Power efficiency: useful power output ÷ total power input. Same formula, same rules — just using watts instead of joules.
