⚡ Potential Difference, Current and Resistance

Spec 4.2.1.3 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Burger Van — Mr Badmus' Circuit

Mr Badmus runs a burger van.

The number of burgers he MAKES per minute is the Potential Difference (p.d.) — the driving force.

The waiters and waitresses DELIVERING burgers are the charge carriers (electrons).

How many burgers get delivered per second is the Current.

A congested street, a broken-down van blocking the road, a queue of people — that's Resistance.

More congestion = fewer burgers delivered = lower current.

What is Potential Difference?

Potential difference (p.d.) is the energy given to each unit of charge by the power supply.

Don't call it 'voltage' in your exam — call it potential difference!

Measured in Volts (V) using a voltmeter in PARALLEL.

What is Resistance?

Resistance is anything that opposes the flow of current.

The higher the resistance, the less current flows for the same p.d.

Think: a narrow road vs a wide road — fewer cars get through the narrow one.

Measured in Ohms (Ω).

Ohm's Law

A component is ohmic if its resistance stays constant when temperature stays constant.

On an I-V graph, an ohmic conductor gives a straight line through the origin.

A curved I-V graph means resistance is changing — non-ohmic.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Students write 'voltage' instead of 'potential difference' and lose marks. Also — never put a voltmeter in series or an ammeter in parallel. The voltmeter goes ACROSS (parallel), the ammeter goes IN THE LINE (series).
