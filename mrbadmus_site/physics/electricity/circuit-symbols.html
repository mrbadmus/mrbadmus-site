📖 In-Depth Theory

What is a circuit diagram?

A circuit diagram is a map of an electrical circuit.

Instead of drawing real components, we use standard symbols.

Every physicist in the world uses the same symbols — so anyone can read your diagram.

How to draw a good circuit diagram

Always use a ruler — no freehand wiggly lines!

Components sit on straight horizontal or vertical lines.

Corners are right angles — like a rectangle.

Label components with their values where given (e.g. '6 Ω').

Series vs Parallel — spot the difference

Series: one single loop — like a one-way street with no junctions.

Parallel: the wire splits into branches — like a road that forks then rejoins.

Ammeter and Voltmeter — where do they go?

Ammeter — measures current. Goes IN SERIES (in the main loop).

Voltmeter — measures potential difference. Goes IN PARALLEL (branching across the component).

Swap these and you'll get zero readings or blow the meter!

⚠️ Common Mistake

Students often draw voltmeters in series and ammeters in parallel — the wrong way round! Remember: Ammeter = in the main line (series). Voltmeter = branching across (parallel).
