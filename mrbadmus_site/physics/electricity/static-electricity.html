⚡ Static Electricity

Spec 4.2.5 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

How Static Charge Builds Up

When two insulators are rubbed together, electrons transfer from one to the other.

The object that GAINS electrons becomes negatively charged.

The object that LOSES electrons becomes positively charged.

Only electrons move — protons are locked in the nucleus and never transfer.

Attraction and Repulsion

Like charges REPEL — two negative objects push away from each other.

Unlike charges ATTRACT — positive and negative pull towards each other.

Think: North poles of two magnets pushing apart — same idea with same charges.

Electric Fields

An electric field is the region around a charged object where another charge experiences a force.

Field lines point FROM positive TO negative — like an arrow showing the direction a positive charge would move.

Closer field lines = stronger field.

Between two parallel plates: uniform field — evenly spaced parallel lines.

Real-World Uses

Inkjet printers: charged ink droplets are deflected by electric fields to land in the right place.

Electrostatic paint sprayers: paint droplets are charged, car body is earthed — paint is attracted evenly.

Electrostatic precipitators: remove dust and soot from factory chimneys using charged plates.

Defibrillators: deliver a controlled electric shock to restart the heart.

Dangers of Static

Fuel tankers are earthed before refuelling — a spark from static could ignite fuel vapour.

Aircraft build up charge during flight — earthing strips discharge them safely on landing.

Electronic components can be destroyed by static discharge — handled with anti-static mats and wristbands.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Students say 'protons transfer during charging by friction' — they DON'T. Only electrons move. Protons are in the nucleus and can't transfer. Always say 'electrons are transferred from X to Y'.

F = EQ (Higher)
E = V/d (Higher)

Example 1 — Explaining charge transfer

A plastic rod is rubbed with a cloth. Explain why the rod becomes negatively charged.

F

Identify what transfers during friction charging

I

Electrons (negatively charged) transfer from the cloth to the plastic rod

F

Protons cannot move — they are fixed in the nucleus

A

The rod gains electrons → gains negative charge. The cloth loses electrons → becomes positively charged

Example 2 — Electric field strength (Higher)

Two parallel plates are 0.02 m apart. The p.d. between them is 500 V. Calculate the electric field strength.

F

E = V ÷ d

I

E = 500 ÷ 0.02

F

No unit conversion needed

A

E = 25,000 V/m = 25 kV/m

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Electric field strength: E = V ÷ d (p.d. between plates ÷ distance apart). Force on a charge: F = EQ. Stronger field or larger charge = larger force.
