⚡ Charge, Current and Time

Spec 4.2.1.2 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

Think of it like a water pipe — but cooler

Imagine Mr Badmus has a burger van.

The burgers being delivered are the CHARGE — each burger is a tiny electron.

The number of burgers delivered every second is the CURRENT.

More burgers per second = bigger current.

The total number of burgers delivered over the whole shift = total charge.

What is electric current?

Current is the rate of flow of charge — how much charge passes a point every second.

In metal conductors, electrons are the charge carriers.

Current is measured in Amperes (A) using an ammeter in series.

What is electric charge?

Charge is the 'stuff' that flows — it's carried by electrons.

One electron carries a tiny charge of 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C.

Charge is measured in Coulombs (C).

⚠️ Common Mistake

Time MUST be in seconds before you use Q = It. If the question gives minutes, multiply by 60 first. Forgetting to convert is the single most common error in this topic — examiners know it and test it deliberately!

Example 2 — Find current (I)

A total charge of 450 C flows through a circuit in 1.5 minutes. Calculate the current.

F

I = Q ÷ t

I

Q = 450 C, t = 1.5 min → convert: 1.5 × 60 = 90 s

F

I = 450 ÷ 90

A

I = 5 A

Example 3 — Find time (t)

A current of 0.5 A delivers a charge of 180 C. How long does this take? Give your answer in minutes.

F

t = Q ÷ I

I

t = 180 ÷ 0.5

F

t = 360 s → convert to minutes: 360 ÷ 60

A

t = 6 minutes
