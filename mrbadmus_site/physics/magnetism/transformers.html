⚡ Electromagnetic Induction and Transformers

Spec 4.7.3 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

Electromagnetic Induction

When a conductor moves through a magnetic field (or a field changes through a conductor), an EMF (voltage) is induced.

This is the reverse of the motor effect — instead of current + field → force, we have motion + field → current.

No movement = no induction. The field must be CHANGING relative to the conductor.

How to Increase Induced EMF

Move faster (increase rate of change of field).

Use a stronger magnet (stronger field).

Use more turns of wire in the coil.

More turns = more of the field passes through more wire = bigger EMF.

Transformers

A transformer changes the voltage of an AC supply.

Primary coil: input voltage applied here.

Secondary coil: output voltage taken from here.

Iron core: links the two coils magnetically (flux linkage).

Step-UP transformer: more turns on secondary → higher output voltage.

Step-DOWN transformer: fewer turns on secondary → lower output voltage.

Transformers only work with AC — because AC creates a changing magnetic field in the core.

Why AC — Not DC?

DC creates a constant magnetic field in the iron core.

A constant field does NOT induce an EMF in the secondary coil.

AC creates a continuously changing field → continuously induced EMF in secondary.

This is why the mains supply is AC — so transformers can work throughout the National Grid.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Transformers only work with AC — NEVER DC. Also: a step-up transformer increases voltage but DECREASES current (power in = power out for 100% efficiency). Students often think step-up increases both voltage and current — wrong!

📐 Variables
VpPrimary voltage (Vp) is measured in Volts (V)
VsSecondary voltage (Vs) is measured in Volts (V)
NpPrimary turns (Np) is measured in no unit ()
NsSecondary turns (Ns) is measured in no unit ()
IpPrimary current (Ip) is measured in Amperes (A)
IsSecondary current (Is) is measured in Amperes (A)
📐 Key Equations
Vp/Vs = Np/Ns
Vp × Ip = Vs × Is (100% efficient)
⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Example 1 — Find secondary voltage

A transformer has 200 primary turns and 1000 secondary turns. Primary voltage = 230 V. Calculate the secondary voltage.

F

Vp/Vs = Np/Ns → Vs = Vp × Ns/Np

I

Vs = 230 × 1000/200 = 230 × 5

F

No unit conversion needed

A

Vs = 1150 V (step-up transformer)

Example 2 — Find secondary current (100% efficient)

Primary: 230 V, 10 A. Secondary: 2300 V. What is the secondary current?

F

Vp × Ip = Vs × Is → Is = (Vp × Ip)/Vs

I

Is = (230 × 10)/2300 = 2300/2300

F

No unit conversion needed

A

Is = 1 A (higher voltage = lower current)

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Transformer efficiency = (Vs × Is)/(Vp × Ip) × 100%. Real transformers lose some energy as heat in the coils and core due to resistance and eddy currents.
