⚡ Transverse and Longitudinal Waves

Spec 4.6.1 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

What is a Wave?

A wave transfers energy from one place to another WITHOUT transferring matter.

The particles (or field) oscillate (vibrate) — but they don't travel with the wave.

Think of a Mexican wave in a stadium — the fans move up and down, but the wave travels around the stadium.

Transverse Waves

The oscillation is PERPENDICULAR (at right angles) to the direction of wave travel.

Think: like shaking a rope — your hand moves up and down, but the wave moves along.

Examples: all electromagnetic waves (light, X-rays, radio), water waves, waves on a string.

Can travel through vacuum (space) — this is how light reaches us from the Sun.

Longitudinal Waves

The oscillation is PARALLEL to the direction of wave travel.

Think: like pushing and pulling a slinky — compressions and rarefactions.

Compression: particles pushed together (high pressure).

Rarefaction: particles pulled apart (low pressure).

Examples: sound waves, ultrasound, seismic P-waves.

Need a medium (matter) to travel through — cannot travel through vacuum.

Wave Properties

Amplitude: maximum displacement from rest position (how 'big' the wave is).

Wavelength (λ): distance from one crest to the next (or compression to compression).

Frequency (f): number of complete waves per second — measured in Hertz (Hz).

Period (T): time for one complete wave — T = 1/f.

Wave speed (v): how fast the wave travels — v = fλ.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Longitudinal waves cannot travel through a vacuum — they need particles to compress and rarefy. ONLY transverse waves (electromagnetic) travel through space. Sound is longitudinal — that's why space is silent.

📐 Variables
vWave speed (v) is measured in Metres per second (m/s)
fFrequency (f) is measured in Hertz (Hz)
λWavelength (λ) is measured in Metres (m)
TPeriod (T) is measured in Seconds (s)
AAmplitude (A) is measured in Metres (m)
📐 Key Equations
v = fλ
T = 1/f
📌 Key Note

Sound is longitudinal. Light is transverse. Sound cannot travel in a vacuum.
