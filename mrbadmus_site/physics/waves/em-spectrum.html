⚡ The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Spec 4.6.2 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

What All EM Waves Have in Common

All electromagnetic waves:

→ Are transverse waves.

→ Travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum (the speed of light).

→ Transfer energy.

→ Can travel through vacuum (space).

→ Are part of a continuous spectrum from radio waves to gamma rays.

The Spectrum — Low to High Frequency

Radio waves → Microwaves → Infrared → Visible light → Ultraviolet → X-rays → Gamma rays.

Low frequency end: long wavelength, low energy, less dangerous.

High frequency end: short wavelength, high energy, more dangerous (ionising).

Mnemonic: 'Rabbits Mate In Vast Unexplored X-ray Gardens'

Uses of Each Type

Radio: broadcasting, communications, MRI scanners.

Microwaves: cooking, satellite communications, mobile phones.

Infrared: thermal imaging, remote controls, optical fibres, grills.

Visible: sight, photography, lasers.

UV: sterilisation, detecting forged banknotes, tanning.

X-rays: medical imaging, airport security, cancer treatment.

Gamma: cancer treatment (radiotherapy), sterilising medical equipment, food irradiation.

Hazards

Ionising radiation (UV, X-ray, gamma) can remove electrons from atoms → damages DNA → cancer.

Infrared: skin burns from excessive heat.

Microwaves: internal heating of body tissue.

Radio waves: generally considered safe at normal intensities.

Higher frequency = more energy per photon = more dangerous.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Students list the spectrum in the wrong order. Remember: Radio → Micro → Infra → Visible → Ultra → X-ray → Gamma. Low to high frequency, long to short wavelength. Gamma has the SHORTEST wavelength and HIGHEST frequency.

📐 Key Equations
v = fλ (same for all EM waves)
v = 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum
📌 Key Note

All EM waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum. Higher frequency = shorter wavelength = more energy = more dangerous.
