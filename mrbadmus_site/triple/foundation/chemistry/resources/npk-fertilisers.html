🧪 Production and Uses of NPK Fertilisers

Spec 4.10.4.2 📗 Foundation
📖 In-Depth Theory

Why Fertilisers Are Needed

Plants need MINERAL IONS from the soil for healthy growth.
The three most important elements for plant growth:
NITROGEN (N):
Needed for protein synthesis → healthy leaves and stems.
Deficiency: yellow leaves, stunted growth.
PHOSPHORUS (P):
Needed for root development and energy transfer (ATP).
Deficiency: poor root growth, purple leaves.
POTASSIUM (K):
Needed for enzyme function and photosynthesis.
Deficiency: poor fruit development, brown leaf edges.
NPK FERTILISERS contain compounds of all three elements.
Used to REPLENISH soil minerals removed by crops.
Without fertilisers: soil becomes depleted → lower crop yields → food insecurity.

Manufacturing NPK Fertilisers

NPK fertilisers are manufactured using INTEGRATED INDUSTRIAL PROCESSES — several reactions are linked.
KEY STARTING MATERIAL: AMMONIA (from Haber process)
NITROGEN COMPOUNDS:
Ammonium nitrate (NH₄NO₃): ammonia + nitric acid.
Ammonium sulfate: ammonia + sulfuric acid.
Ammonium phosphate: ammonia + phosphoric acid.
PHOSPHORUS COMPOUNDS:
Phosphoric acid made from phosphate rock + sulfuric acid.
Superphosphate: calcium phosphate (from rock) treated with H₂SO₄.
POTASSIUM COMPOUNDS:
Potassium chloride or potassium sulfate — mined from natural mineral deposits.
No synthesis needed — extracted and purified.
BLENDING:
Different salts blended in specific ratios to give the required NPK formulation.
Different crops and soil types need different N:P:K ratios.
Fertiliser bags labelled with N:P:K ratio (e.g. 20:10:10).

Environmental Issues with Fertilisers

EUTROPHICATION:
Excess fertiliser washed into waterways by rain (LEACHING).
Nitrogen and phosphorus stimulate algal growth (algal bloom).
Algae block light → aquatic plants die.
Bacteria decompose dead plants → use up oxygen.
Fish and other organisms die — DEAD ZONE.
NITRATE IN DRINKING WATER:
Excess nitrate in groundwater can contaminate drinking water supplies.
High nitrate in infants: 'blue baby syndrome' (methaemoglobinaemia) — nitrate reduces blood oxygen.
EU limit: 50 mg/L nitrate in drinking water.
ACID RAIN:
Ammonium fertilisers can be oxidised in soil to nitrate → releases H⁺ ions → soil acidification.
REDUCING ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT:
Precision agriculture — apply only what is needed.
Slow-release fertilisers — coated to release gradually.
Organic farming — using manure instead of synthetic fertilisers.
Buffer zones — vegetated strips near waterways to absorb runoff.
⚠️ Common Mistake

NPK fertilisers contain nitrogen, phosphorus AND potassium — not just nitrogen. The Haber process makes AMMONIA, which is then used to make ammonium salts for fertilisers. Potassium comes from MINING, not synthesis. Eutrophication is caused by excess nutrients (N and P) entering waterways, not just nitrates.

📐 Key Equations
NH₃ + HNO₃ → NH₄NO₃ (ammonium nitrate fertiliser)
2NH₃ + H₂SO₄ → (NH₄)₂SO₄ (ammonium sulfate fertiliser)
📌 Key Note

N = proteins/leaves, P = roots/ATP, K = enzymes/photosynthesis. NPK fertilisers: ammonium salts (from NH₃ + acids), potassium from mining. Manufacturing: integrated processes using Haber ammonia + acids. Environmental issues: eutrophication (leaching → algal bloom → deoxygenation), nitrate in drinking water. Precision farming reduces impact.
