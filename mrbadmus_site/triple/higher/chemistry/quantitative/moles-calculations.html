🧪 Moles, Mass and Relative Formula Mass

Spec 4.3.1 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

Relative Formula Mass (Mr)

Mr = sum of all relative atomic masses (Ar) in the formula.
H₂O: (2×1) + 16 = 18.
CO₂: 12 + (2×16) = 44.
NaCl: 23 + 35.5 = 58.5.
Mr has NO UNITS — it's a ratio.

The Mole

One mole = 6.02 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro's number).
1 mole of any element weighs its Ar in grams.
1 mole of any compound weighs its Mr in grams.
Formula: moles = mass ÷ Mr.

Using Moles in Reactions

A balanced equation tells you the mole ratio of reactants and products.
Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O means 2 mol H₂ reacts with 1 mol O₂ to give 2 mol H₂O.
Scale up or down using the ratio.
Higher: limiting reactant = the one that runs out first and determines yield.

Conservation of Mass

Total mass of reactants = total mass of products.
If mass appears to decrease: a gas was produced and escaped.
If mass appears to increase: a gas from the atmosphere was absorbed (e.g. burning magnesium takes O₂ from air).
Always account for all products, including gases.
⚠️ Common Mistake

Mr has NO UNITS. Mass must be in GRAMS before using n = m ÷ Mr. If given in kg, convert first (×1000).

⚽ FIFA Worked Examples
Example 1 — Calculate moles

Calculate the moles in 44 g of CO₂. (C=12, O=16)

F

n = m ÷ Mr

I

Mr(CO₂) = 12 + 32 = 44. n = 44 ÷ 44

F

Mass already in grams

A

n = 1 mol

Example 2 — Calculate mass

What mass of NaCl (Mr=58.5) is in 0.5 mol?

F

m = n × Mr

I

m = 0.5 × 58.5

F

No conversion needed

A

m = 29.25 g

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Moles of gas at RTP: n = volume (dm³) ÷ 24. One mole of any gas = 24 dm³ at room temperature and pressure.
