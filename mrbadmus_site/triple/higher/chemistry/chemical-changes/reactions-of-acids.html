Reactions of Acids with Metals and Bases

Spec 5.4.2.1–5.4.2.2 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

What Are Acids and Bases?

An ACID is a substance that produces H⁺ ions (hydrogen ions) when dissolved in water.
Common acids: hydrochloric acid (HCl), sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄), nitric acid (HNO₃).
A BASE is a substance that neutralises an acid. Bases are metal oxides or metal hydroxides.
An ALKALI is a base that dissolves in water — it produces OH⁻ ions in solution.
Common alkalis: sodium hydroxide (NaOH), potassium hydroxide (KOH), calcium hydroxide (Ca(OH)₂).
All acid reactions produce a SALT — formed from the positive metal ion and the negative ion from the acid:
Hydrochloric acid → CHLORIDE salts (e.g. NaCl, MgCl₂)
Sulfuric acid → SULFATE salts (e.g. ZnSO₄, CuSO₄)
Nitric acid → NITRATE salts (e.g. Ca(NO₃)₂, KNO₃)

Reactions with Metals and Metal Oxides

ACID + METAL → SALT + HYDROGEN:
Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂
Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂
Fe + 2HCl → FeCl₂ + H₂
Test for hydrogen: lit splint → squeaky pop.
Only metals ABOVE hydrogen in reactivity series react with dilute acids.
Copper, silver, gold — NO reaction.
ACID + METAL OXIDE → SALT + WATER:
Metal oxides are BASES — they neutralise acids.
CuO + 2HCl → CuCl₂ + H₂O
ZnO + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂O
Fe₂O₃ + 3H₂SO₄ → Fe₂(SO₄)₃ + 3H₂O
Observation: the metal oxide powder DISSOLVES in the acid as the reaction proceeds. The solution often changes colour as the metal salt forms (e.g. blue for copper sulfate).

Reactions with Hydroxides and Carbonates

ACID + METAL HYDROXIDE → SALT + WATER (neutralisation):
NaOH + HCl → NaCl + H₂O
2KOH + H₂SO₄ → K₂SO₄ + 2H₂O
Ca(OH)₂ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + 2H₂O
This is a NEUTRALISATION reaction — H⁺ from acid combines with OH⁻ from alkali to form water.
ACID + METAL CARBONATE → SALT + WATER + CARBON DIOXIDE:
CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂
Na₂CO₃ + H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + H₂O + CO₂
ZnCO₃ + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂
Test for CO₂: bubble through limewater → turns milky/cloudy.
Observation: fizzing/effervescence as CO₂ gas is produced. The solid carbonate dissolves as the reaction proceeds.
Summary of products:
Acid + metal → salt + hydrogen
Acid + metal oxide → salt + water
Acid + metal hydroxide → salt + water
Acid + metal carbonate → salt + water + carbon dioxide
⚠️ Common Mistake

Acid + metal carbonate produces THREE products: salt + water + carbon DIOXIDE. Students often forget the water or forget the CO₂. Also: the SALT name comes from the METAL and the ACID — sulfuric acid always gives sulfate salts, hydrochloric acid gives chloride salts, nitric acid gives nitrate salts.

📐 Key Equations
Acid + metal → salt + hydrogen
Acid + metal oxide → salt + water
Acid + metal hydroxide → salt + water
Acid + metal carbonate → salt + water + carbon dioxide
Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂
CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂
📌 Key Note

Acid + metal → salt + H₂ (metals above H only). Acid + metal oxide → salt + H₂O. Acid + metal hydroxide → salt + H₂O (neutralisation). Acid + carbonate → salt + H₂O + CO₂. H₂: squeaky pop. CO₂: turns limewater milky. Salt name = metal + acid anion.
