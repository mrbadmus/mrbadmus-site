⚡ The Solar System and Life Cycle of Stars

Spec 4.8.1 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Solar System

The Sun is at the centre — a medium-sized star.
Eight planets orbit the Sun (plus dwarf planets like Pluto).
Order from Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.
Mnemonic: 'My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos'.
Gravity keeps planets in orbit — centripetal force directed toward the Sun.

Beyond the Solar System

Our Solar System sits in the Milky Way galaxy.
The Milky Way contains around 200–400 billion stars.
The universe contains hundreds of billions of galaxies.
Light-year: the distance light travels in one year ≈ 9.5 × 10¹⁵ m.
The observable universe is about 46 billion light-years in radius.

Life Cycle of a Small/Medium Star (like our Sun)

Nebula (gas and dust cloud) → Protostar (gravity pulls material together, heats up)
→ Main sequence star (hydrogen fusion, stable for billions of years)
→ Red giant (hydrogen runs out, outer layers expand)
→ Planetary nebula (outer layers shed)
→ White dwarf (dense core remains, cools slowly).
Our Sun is currently a main sequence star — about halfway through its life.

Life Cycle of a Massive Star

Nebula → Protostar → Main sequence star (shorter life — burns fuel faster)
→ Red supergiant (much larger than red giant)
→ Supernova (catastrophic explosion — elements heavier than iron created here!)
→ Either: Neutron star (incredibly dense — mass of Sun in city-sized ball)
→ Or: Black hole (if mass is large enough — gravity so strong light cannot escape).
⚠️ Common Mistake

A white dwarf and a neutron star are NOT the same. White dwarfs come from small/medium stars (like our Sun). Neutron stars come from massive stars after a supernova. Black holes form from the MOST massive stars. Get the sequence right!

📌 Key Note

All elements heavier than iron are formed in supernova explosions — including gold, silver and uranium on Earth.
