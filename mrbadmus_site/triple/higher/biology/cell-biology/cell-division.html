🌿 Cell Division — Mitosis and Stem Cells

Spec 4.1.4 📙 Higher
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Cell Cycle and Mitosis

Mitosis = cell division producing two genetically identical daughter cells.

Used for: growth, repair of tissues, replacement of worn-out cells.

Before division: DNA is replicated (copied) — so each new cell gets a full set.

Stages: interphase (growth + DNA replication) → mitosis (division) → two daughter cells.

Each daughter cell has the same chromosome number as the parent cell.

Cancer — Uncontrolled Cell Division

Cancer occurs when cells divide uncontrollably.

Mutation in genes that control the cell cycle → cells keep dividing → tumour.

Benign tumour: doesn't spread. Malignant tumour: spreads to other tissues (metastasis).

Treatment: surgery, radiotherapy (gamma rays), chemotherapy (drugs).

Stem Cells

Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that can divide and specialise.

Embryonic stem cells: found in early embryos — can become any cell type (totipotent).

Adult stem cells: found in bone marrow etc — more limited range of cell types.

Therapeutic use: treating leukaemia, type 1 diabetes, Parkinson's disease.

Ethical issues: embryonic stem cell use destroys embryos — significant debate.

Cell Differentiation

As organisms develop, cells differentiate — become specialised for specific functions.

Red blood cells: no nucleus, biconcave disc — maximises oxygen carrying.

Neurone: long axon with myelin sheath — fast signal transmission.

Root hair cell: long projection — large surface area for water and mineral absorption.

Sperm cell: streamlined, lots of mitochondria, acrosome — adapted to reach and fertilise egg.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Mitosis produces TWO identical daughter cells with the SAME chromosome number as the parent. Meiosis (Higher) produces FOUR genetically different cells with HALF the chromosome number. Don't confuse them!

📌 Key Note

Mitosis = growth and repair (genetically identical). Used for asexual reproduction too.
