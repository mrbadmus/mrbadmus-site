Spec 4.4 Paper 1
📋 What You Need to Know
  • ● Photosynthesis equation — 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  • ● Limiting factors of photosynthesis — light, CO₂, temperature
  • ● Uses of glucose
  • ● Aerobic respiration — C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O
  • ● Anaerobic respiration — humans and yeast
  • ● Response to exercise — heart rate, breathing rate
📐 Key Equations
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O (aerobic)
glucose → lactic acid (anaerobic, humans)
glucose → ethanol + CO₂ (fermentation)

🎯 Quick Quiz
1. What are the products of aerobic respiration?
Correct! Aerobic respiration: glucose + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water (+ energy) ✅
Not quite. Aerobic respiration produces carbon dioxide and water. Lactic acid is from anaerobic respiration in animals.
2. Which factor is NOT a limiting factor of photosynthesis?
Correct! Soil pH is not a direct limiting factor. The three main limiting factors are light, CO₂ and temperature ✅
Not quite. Soil pH doesn't directly limit photosynthesis. Light intensity, CO₂ concentration and temperature are the three main limiting factors.
3. In anaerobic respiration in yeast, what are the products?
Correct! Yeast fermentation: glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide ✅
Not quite. Yeast produces ethanol and CO₂ in anaerobic respiration. Lactic acid is produced in human muscle cells.
