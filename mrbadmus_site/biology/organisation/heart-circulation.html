⚡ The Heart and Circulatory System

Spec 4.2.3 Paper 1
📖 In-Depth Theory

The Double Circulatory System

Humans have a DOUBLE circulatory system — two loops.

Loop 1: heart → lungs → heart (picks up O₂, drops off CO₂).

Loop 2: heart → body → heart (delivers O₂, collects CO₂).

Double system maintains high pressure for efficient delivery to all body tissues.

Structure of the Heart

Four chambers: right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, left ventricle.

Right side: pumps deoxygenated blood to lungs.

Left side: pumps oxygenated blood to body — thicker walls (higher pressure needed).

Valves prevent backflow: atrioventricular valves between atria and ventricles, semilunar valves in arteries.

Natural pacemaker (SA node) controls heart rate.

Blood Vessels

Arteries: carry blood AWAY from heart. Thick muscular walls, small lumen, no valves. High pressure.

Veins: carry blood TOWARDS heart. Thin walls, large lumen, VALVES to prevent backflow. Low pressure.

Capillaries: one cell thick — allow exchange of O₂, CO₂, glucose, waste between blood and tissues.

Blood Components

Red blood cells: carry O₂ using haemoglobin. Biconcave, no nucleus.

White blood cells: immune response — phagocytes engulf pathogens, lymphocytes produce antibodies.

Platelets: small fragments — involved in blood clotting.

Plasma: liquid — transports glucose, CO₂, urea, hormones, heat.

⚠️ Common Mistake

The LEFT ventricle has thicker walls than the right — it pumps to the WHOLE BODY (further distance, higher pressure needed). The right pumps only to the lungs (nearby). Students often get this backwards.

📌 Key Note

Arteries = Away from heart. Veins = towards heart. Capillaries = exchange sites.

⭐ Higher Tier Only

Coronary heart disease: fatty deposits (atherosclerosis) narrow coronary arteries → reduced blood flow to heart muscle → angina or heart attack. Treatments: statins, stents, bypass surgery.
